Can African economies afford a lockdown?

November 20th 2020 John Isobar

Reading Time: 5 minutes

Many African countries have faced a dire situation when it comes to managing the Corona virus since its outbreak in the better part of 2020. With many have unequipped health infrastructures, Covid-19 was nothing but untimely. African countries were already dealing with a fragile economy and the virus only seemed to complicate the plight of individual citizens.

At the eye of the storm is the glaring fact that job losses have spiraled out of control even without a serious lockdown in place. A country like Kenya, for instance, had to play a balancing act between disease control and livelihoods. However at least 770,000 Kenyans lost their jobs between January and March this year, new data from the Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) reported. The country has already shifted its efforts to home-based care as those who are infected surpass the capacity of local hospitals.
